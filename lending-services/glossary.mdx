This contains the common terms and their definitions encountered when using the Lending Services API.

### Common Mortgage Terms

**Adjudication**
The process of determining whether a borrower can afford a given mortgage offer. The API calculates the borrower's GDS and TDS ratios and compares them against each lender's specific thresholds. Offers that exceed a lender's limits are excluded from the response.

**Amortization**
The total length of time over which a mortgage is repaid, expressed in months. For example, a 25-year amortization is 300 months. This is distinct from the mortgage term. Offers in the response are grouped by amortization period.

**Bona Fide Clause**
A mortgage feature that restricts the lender from calling or renegotiating the mortgage unless the property is sold. Indicated by `hasBonafideClause` on an offer's feature flags.

**Bridge Financing**
A short-term loan that allows a borrower to cover the gap between purchasing a new property and receiving proceeds from selling an existing one. Indicated by `hasBridgeFinancing`.

**Commission**
The fee Perch earns when a mortgage is funded through a Lender. The `CommissionInfo` input object allows callers to specify minimum commission thresholds and maximum buydown limits; the `CommissionInformation` object in the response reports the gross commission, buydown rate applied, and resulting buydown cost where applicable.

**Deal Purpose**
The nature of the mortgage transaction. Supported values are `PURCHASE`, `RENEWAL`, `REFINANCE`, and `SWITCH`. Lenders may only support certain transaction types; this is used during lender filtering.

**Debt Service Ratios (GDS / TDS)**
Standard Canadian mortgage qualification metrics. Lenders set their own GDS and TDS thresholds; qualified endpoints enforce these limits.

**Down Payment**
The upfront amount the borrower contributes toward the purchase price. Affects the loan-to-value ratio, which determines insurance requirements and lender eligibility.

**HELOC (Home Equity Line of Credit)**
A revolving credit facility secured against property equity, often attached to an existing mortgage. A readvanceable HELOC allows the borrower to re-borrow repaid principal. Indicated by `hasReadvanceableHeloc`.

**Insured Status**
Describes whether a mortgage is covered by default insurance. The API returns one of four statuses:
- `INSURED`: mortgage is insured; down payment is less than 20%
- `INSURABLE`: mortgage meets insurer criteria but is not currently insured (down payment is 20%+)
- `UNINSURABLE`: mortgage does not meet insurer criteria (e.g., purchase price over $1.5M, refinance)
- `INSURED QUICK CLOSE`: insured mortgage with an expedited closing premium applied

**Insurance Premium**
A one-time premium charged on insured mortgages, added to the principal. The amount is based on the loan-to-value (LTV) ratio and is set by the mortgage insurer.

**Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)**
The ratio of the mortgage amount to the property's appraised or purchase value. LTV determines whether mortgage insurance is required (above 80% LTV = insured) and influences lender eligibility and product availability.

**Offer**
A single mortgage product from a specific lender, for a given term and rate type. Each offer includes the interest rate, payment details, fees, commission breakdown, feature flags, and insured status.

**Offer Group**
A collection of offers that share the same amortization period, rate type, and term length. The `/qualified/all` and `/qualified/lowest-rates` responses return offers grouped this way, sorted by effective rate within each group.


**Portability**
A mortgage feature that allows the borrower to transfer their existing mortgage — including rate and terms — to a new property when they move, avoiding prepayment penalties. Indicated by `hasPortability`.

**Assumability**
A feature that allows a buyer to take over the seller's existing mortgage, including its rate and remaining term. Indicated by `hasAssumability`.

**Prepayment Penalty**
A fee charged when a borrower breaks or pays off their mortgage before the end of the term. The API returns penalty details including the penalty type (`GREATEST` or `LEAST` of the applicable calculations) and the component breakdown (interest, IRD, or fixed amount).

**Principal**
The outstanding loan amount, excluding any insurance premium. 

**Product Category**
A grouping of mortgage offers by rate type and term, used by the `/qualified/lowest-rates` endpoint to return the best offer per category (e.g., fixed 5-year, variable 3-year).

**Property Valuation**
The assessed value of the target property. The API accepts three valuation types
- `purchase`: The Purchase Price details of the house when it was acquired, or the cost of the house the borrower is looking to purchase (if a new buy)
- `estimated`: A Perch estimated value of the property
- `appraisal`: The amount the property was appraised for if refinancing.
The valuation influences LTV calculations and lender eligibility.

**Rate Type**
The structure of the interest rate applied to a mortgage. The API supports three types:
- `FIXED`: rate is locked for the full term
- `VARIABLE`: rate floats with the lender's prime rate; payments may vary
- `ADJUSTABLE`: rate floats with prime, but payments adjust automatically to maintain amortization schedule

**Request ID**
A unique correlation identifier returned on every response (`requestId`) and echoed in the `X-Trace-Id` response header. Include this in any support requests for traceability.

**Switch**
A transaction type where a borrower moves their existing mortgage from one lender to another at renewal, typically to obtain a better rate, without changing the loan amount.

**Term**
The length of the current mortgage contract, expressed in months. This is distinct from the amortization period. Offers can be filtered by term using the `terms` filter parameter.
