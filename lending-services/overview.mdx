
Welcome to the Perch Lending Services API, this is the centralized engine powering **Mortgage Offer** generation across Perch's platform and partner integrations.

This API evaluates borrower profiles against lender policies, and product catalogues and returns ranked mortgage offers; the service runs a live evaluation that filters for lenders, calculating rates and commissions, applying buydown logic, and adjudicating each offer against the borrower's debt servicing capacity.

<Note>All monetary values returned are in **Canadian Dollars (CAD)**.</Note>


---

## What the API Does

At its core, the Lending Services API answers one question: *what mortgage offers can this borrower get, and at what rate?*

When a request is made, the API runs the borrower's profile through a sequential pipeline:

1. **Lender Filtering** - Narrows the pool to lenders whose policies are compatible with the borrower's province, credit score, transaction type, property type, and requested features etc.
2. **Rates & Commissions** - Calculates the applicable interest rate and Perch commission for each eligible lender.
3. **Buydown** - Where applicable, redirects a portion of commission to reduce the borrower's offered rate below the market benchmark. (*Should this be included?*)
4. **Adjudication** - Validates whether the borrower can service the debt using GDS and TDS ratios, compared against each lender's specific thresholds.
5. **Offer Grouping & Ranking** - Returns surviving offers grouped by amortization, rate type, and term, sorted by effective rate (low to high).

## Qualified vs. Unqualified Endpoints

The API exposes two modes of offer retrieval, this guide explains the difference and provides recommendations on when to use either of them.

### Qualified (`/qualified/..`)

Qualified endpoints run the full pipeline (including adjudication). 

Offers returned here are ones the borrower has been assessed against i.e., their income, existing debts, and property costs are evaluated to compute Debt Servicing Ratios (GDS & TDS); these ratios are then compared against each lender's thresholds to determine what offers the borrower can actually service.

**Use qualified endpoints when:**
- You are presenting offers to a specific, identified borrower with known financial details
- You need to show only what a borrower is eligible for (e.g., in an application flow or rate comparison tool)
- Accuracy and lender compliance are required e.g., before a borrower proceeds to a mortgage application


**Real-world example:** A borrower on your platform has submitted their income, existing liabilities, and property details. You call `POST /api/v1/offers/qualified/lowest-rates` to surface the best available rate per product category (fixed 5-year, variable, etc.) that this specific person qualifies for. The response reflects what they could realistically be approved for.

### Unqualified (`/unqualified/..`)

Unqualified endpoints consider the borrower's profile attributes e.g., property type, province, down payment, transaction type etc. to apply lender eligibility and product boundaries, but they do **not** restrict offers based on debt servicing ratios (*adjudication*). The borrower's income and existing debts are not used to filter results.

**Use unqualified endpoints when:**
- You want to show indicative or best-available rates without tying results to a specific borrower's financial position
- You are building a rate explorer, pre-qualification widget, or marketing surface where a borrower has not yet provided full financial details
- You want to benchmark what the market looks like for a given property and product type

**Real-world example:** A visitor lands on your marketing site and wants to know what mortgage rates look like today - no account, no application, no financial details entered. You call `POST /api/v1/offers/unqualified/lowest-rate` with the property type, province, and a standard down payment amount to display the best rate currently available across all lenders. The result powers a "Today's Lowest Rate" headline or rate ticker on the page thus giving prospective borrowers a compelling, market-accurate number without requiring them to share any personal financial information.

---

### Choosing the Right Endpoint

| Scenario | Endpoint to Use |
|---|---|
| Full mortgage application with borrower financials | `/qualified/all` or `/qualified/lowest-rates` |
| Show the single best rate a borrower qualifies for | `/qualified/lowest-rate` |
| Rate explorer / marketing surface (no full profile) | `/unqualified/lowest-rate` |
| Comparison tool for best rate per product category based on the borrower's financial position | `/qualified/lowest-rates` |
| Pre-qualification widget with partial borrower data | `/unqualified/lowest-rate` |
